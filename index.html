Abstract

    This paper aims to investigate the potential usage of Black Holes as energy sources via the investigation of several theorised extraction 
    methods from multiple academic and research journals whereby the possibility of each technique was investigated. 
    Finding that according to the laws of physics and the inclusion of the quantum realm then it is realistic to be able to consider the usage of a
    Black Hole as an energy source. However, it was found that despite the possibility, plausibility is another issue due to unavailability at the current 
    time of the necessary technology which will need to be developed specifically for the use of extracting and storing the energy from a Black Hole.

Introduction

       Space and the endless possibilities it holds have been of interest to humans for generations, opportunities offered by Black Holes have been at the 
      forefront of science since the discovery of the first black hole in 1971 (Stuart,2024). The possibility of using Black Holes as an energy source in 
      the future has been greatly contested due to the complexity of the issues, with research into the problem rapidly occurring. Accounting for the
      necessary technological improvements, this paper will examine the viability of using Black Holes as energy sources and the future use they may provide by
      first inspecting the physical potential of the concepts and how this may be conducted, along with the efficiency of the currently theorised processes.
